How do I find an IP Professional?

So, you’ve reviewed your Terrifio™ Digital IP Adivsor Report and now you’re ready to contact an IP Professional.

We suggest the following.

First, you should have an idea in your mind of a budget you can work with. You need to align what you can afford with a budget that your IP Professional can work within to carry out the needed mandate(s).

An IP Professional will need to check for conflicts and will ask for general information in order to do so, including:

  1. a general description of your project (the name of your Project may be a good first step);

  2. your name and the names of your collaborators (if any); and

  3. the name of any parties that may be adverse to you (this could include known competitors for you, for example).

Once your IP Professional “clears conflicts”, you may need to sign an Engagement Letter, which will lay out your obligations to settle accounts.

At this point you’ll be ready to discuss and verify your Project details with your IP Professional, including your Terrifio™ Digital IP Advisor Report.
